We're on a mission to create a first ever solid record label. Alright, maybe not the first ever - cause there are some indie record labels that are run by true legends - but the first large-scale mainstream heavy hitter of the record label.

What do we mean by solid? Well, imagine a label that:

  • Does not fuck over the artists
  • Is owned and led by the artists
  • Is owned and led by the community
  • Is more dedicated to music than to dallah Bill's
  • Is not a part of any secretive, behind the door market rigging

Yeah, that's probably sums it up.

The music industry has been suffering long enough. Sure, we can give it a few more years to turn into even more shit with more artists being ripped off and the quality of the music dropping. Or, we may never launch our label only to find out a few years down the line that someone did it successfully, and smashed the industry to bits. But neither of these options work for us, so LFG fam.

No one really knows when the shittery has begun. Was it the 50's when the labels were the only option for the artists to come up? 80's when the music started to really blow up with new genres right and left? Or was itthe 90's when the labels gave the artists 360 contracts and took their souls and livelihoods instead? Fuck knows, we've not been around long enough to tell. But it is what it is, and it's time to change it.

Remember the good old days when musicians went from bar to bar, from park to high street, from a wedding to a birthday party playing, singing and entertaining the audience? No? Neither do we. Haven't been around long enough.

The most common path to glory and riches for the current generation of aspiring musicians is via social media, talent shows and pre-arranged recorded busking performances. There are far more options these days for said musicians to find success but also far more competition.

The problem is, that a musician now has to become a sound engineer, content creator, graphic designer, photographer, video editor and a promoter, all in one. It's kinda hard to fit than all in, between pouring pints in a pub and driving for uber-eats, not to mention it would be good to throw some peebles into a lake in search of inspiration every now and then.

his is where we come in. We want to help our artist to focus on their craft without having all the other distractions to deal with. We don't need our artists to be big before coming to us. We don't need them to go viral on Tiktok for singing in the Pokemon's outfit while taking a crap. All we want them to have is talent, passion and dedication to creating music rather than dedication to becoming famous.

Also, being in the game for many years now, we thought, fuck it, let's show the middle finger to this rotting industry (i.e. record label businesses). And while there are certainly some good actors out there, fuck'em all, so nobody feels left out. Let's set a new standard. We're triggered and ready to throw hands.

Whass label

At its core, the record label is an investment company that specialises in investing in musicians and music. Much like VCs, Angels, crypto whales and NFTdegens, labels invest in assets that they believe will blow up generating a nice little payday, the only difference being labels put their money into artists instead of some imaginary digital currency.

Why label important

Let's face it, if you're a broke ass guy (notice the spelling, Sound Guy is never broke though, always spare change in his pocket) your tracks may not be production quality, visuals might be lacking that glow, you may not be quite sure how to energise your entire social media audience of 4 and your whole network consists of your dog and your best bro that has always had your back.

Label pays for the production so your masters don't sound like a garage affair, gets you played all over the shop (radio, streaming and also, the actual shop like 7/11) and ensures you get a full TMZ coverage. Can't go wrong on this one.
